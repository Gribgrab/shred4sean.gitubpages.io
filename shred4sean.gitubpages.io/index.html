Our Mission:

The Sean Mahoney Memorial foundation aims to educate and prevent suicide by generating awareness. By creating a culture that understands mental health and that knows when to be proactive, our foundation wants to bring hope to those whom suicide has affected. Our goals are to create event-based experiences and use artistic influence while raising funds to sponsor our awareness and prevention efforts. Proceeds will go towards informational programs that make resources available for those at risk and a scholarship.

    Scholarship

    Scholarship Question: How has your passion allowed you to conquer fears or break down social, physical, and/or cultural barriers?

    Answers from any genre (i.e. poetry, lyrics, fiction, short story, memoir, creative non-fiction, etc.) will be accepted. With the exception of personal essays, all submissions must be accompanied with a short abstract (50-100 words) introducing the piece, its genre, and its function in relation to communicating the applicants story of what they are passionate about.

    Scholarship submissions may not exceed 3,000 words, must be formatted using Times New Roman 12-point font and submitted using .docx or .pdf files. Applicants must also include a copy of their resume with their submission.
